About Me

Born in 1983 in Quanzhou, Fujian, China, raised in Yong'an, and currently settled in New Zealand.

I once navigated the complex data and cross-border logic of the financial industry before anchoring myself in a classroom in the Southern Hemisphere. The shift in geographical coordinates and professional identity is essentially a shift in how I observe the world: from scrutinizing the flow of capital to witnessing the growth of cognition.

🏛️ Pedagogy & Observation#

Currently teaching English and Social Studies at a New Zealand secondary school. In a cross-cultural classroom, I act more as a translator—not just of language, but of mindsets.

📊 Rationality & Accumulation#

My early career in finance left me with a persistence for rigor and the habit of analyzing micro-phenomena from a macro perspective. These accumulations have now transformed into the underlying logic of my instructional design.

🌌 Cosmos & Scale#

Outside of teaching, I assist the Royal Astronomical Society of New Zealand (RASNZ) with administrative affairs. Astronomy has taught me the importance of "humility." Against the backdrop of the starry sky, all professional achievements and worldly gains seem insignificant.
